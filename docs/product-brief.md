# Product Brief — Framing of Central Florida Website

## Vision
A new, modern web presence for Framing of Central Florida that reflects 37+ years of craftsmanship and converts online visitors into in-shop customers. The site should feel like a boutique studio — warm, expert, and tactile — not a generic small-business template.

## Audience
Primary segments, in priority order:
1. **Homeowners** — preserving family photos, art, heirlooms, kids' work.
2. **Collectors of memorabilia** — sports, music, military, autograph, and similar collectible framing.
3. **Commercial clients** — offices, restaurants, hotels, healthcare, retail.
4. **Government organizations** — municipal, state, and federal offices needing certificates, awards, official portraits, and documents framed.

## Value proposition
- 37+ years of custom framing experience, owner-operated.
- Boutique studio with museum-quality craftsmanship.
- Frequently named one of Orlando's top frame shops.
- Specialists in memorabilia and preservation-grade framing.
- Serves both residential and commercial / institutional clients.

## Primary call to action
**Visit the shop.** Every page should make it easy to find the address, hours, phone number, and a map link. Quote requests and phone calls are valuable secondary actions, but the conversion goal is an in-person visit so customers can see materials, discuss the piece, and meet the owner.

## Key differentiators to highlight
- Experience and longevity (since 1993, 37+ years in the trade).
- Owner-operated — the person framing your piece is the person you meet.
- Specialist capabilities for memorabilia and high-value pieces.
- Comfortable working with both individual and institutional buyers.

## Tone & brand
- Warm, confident, craft-forward.
- "From Memories to Masterpieces" is an established tagline — keep it.
- Avoid corporate stock-photo feel; lean on real shop photography and finished pieces.

## Success signals (qualitative, since the site is new)
- New customers report finding the shop online.
- Inbound calls/visits from outside the immediate neighborhood.
- Inquiries from commercial and government buyers, not just homeowners.
- Lower phone-call friction: callers already know hours, location, and rough scope.
